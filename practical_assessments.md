# Practical Assessments & Exercises
## Advanced Digital Marketing Analytics Course

---

## Assessment Framework Overview

### Assessment Philosophy
All assessments are designed to mirror real-world challenges that marketing analytics directors face at Fortune 500 companies. Each assessment combines technical skills, strategic thinking, and executive communication.

### Grading Distribution
- **Continuous Assessment**: 60% (Weekly exercises, case studies, peer reviews)
- **Major Projects**: 40% (Mid-term attribution project, Final capstone presentation)

## Weekly Practical Exercises

### Week 1: Organizational Design & Strategy
**Time Allocation**: 2 hours
**Format**: Individual assignment with peer review

#### Exercise 1.1: Team Structure Design (45 minutes)
**Scenario**: You're the new Director of Marketing Analytics at GlobalTech (Fortune 500 software company, $5B revenue). Design an optimal analytics team structure.

**Deliverables**:
1. **Organizational Chart** (15 minutes)
   - Visual representation of team structure
   - 8-12 roles with clear reporting relationships
   - Skills matrix for each role

2. **Role Definitions** (20 minutes)
   - Job descriptions for 3 key roles
   - Required qualifications and experience
   - Success metrics and KPIs
   - Interview questions for each role

3. **12-Month Hiring Plan** (10 minutes)
   - Prioritized hiring sequence
   - Budget requirements ($2M annual budget)
   - Risk mitigation for key departures

**Evaluation Rubric**:
- Strategic alignment with business needs (25%)
- Organizational design principles (25%)
- Feasibility and budget considerations (25%)
- Clarity of role definitions (25%)

#### Exercise 1.2: Technology ROI Analysis (75 minutes)
**Scenario**: Evaluate three Customer Data Platform options for enterprise implementation.

**Provided Materials**:
- Vendor feature comparison matrices
- Pricing information for 3 CDP solutions
- Current state technology assessment
- Business requirements document

**Deliverables**:
1. **HEART Evaluation Framework** (30 minutes)
   - Complete evaluation for all 3 platforms
   - Weighted scoring based on business priorities
   - Risk assessment for each option

2. **Total Economic Impact Model** (30 minutes)
   - 3-year cost-benefit analysis
   - Revenue impact projections
   - Implementation cost estimates
   - Risk-adjusted ROI calculations

3. **Executive Recommendation** (15 minutes)
   - 2-slide executive summary
   - Clear recommendation with rationale
   - Implementation timeline and milestones

**Success Criteria**:
- Comprehensive analysis using provided frameworks
- Realistic financial projections
- Clear executive-level communication
- Consideration of implementation risks

### Week 2-3: Advanced Attribution & Measurement
**Time Allocation**: 3 hours
**Format**: Technical implementation with business application

#### Exercise 2.1: Attribution Model Development (90 minutes)
**Dataset Provided**: 
- 6 months of customer journey data (anonymized)
- 50,000 customers, 15 touchpoints, $10M revenue
- Multiple conversion events (lead, trial, purchase, renewal)

**Technical Requirements**:
- Python/R environment with provided starter code
- Statistical libraries (scikit-learn, pandas, numpy)
- Visualization tools (matplotlib, plotly)

**Tasks**:
1. **Data Exploration & Preparation** (20 minutes)
   - Analyze customer journey patterns
   - Identify data quality issues
   - Create feature engineering pipeline

2. **Model Implementation** (50 minutes)
   - Build 3 attribution models:
     - Time-decay attribution
     - Shapley value attribution
     - Machine learning-based attribution
   - Compare model performance and insights

3. **Business Impact Analysis** (20 minutes)
   - Calculate budget reallocation recommendations
   - Estimate ROI impact of attribution changes
   - Create executive dashboard mockup

**Deliverables**:
- Jupyter notebook with complete analysis
- Executive summary with key insights
- Budget reallocation recommendations

**Evaluation Criteria**:
- Technical implementation quality (40%)
- Statistical rigor and model validation (30%)
- Business insight and recommendations (20%)
- Code quality and documentation (10%)

#### Exercise 2.2: Marketing Mix Modeling (90 minutes)
**Scenario**: Build MMM for a CPG brand with $50M annual marketing spend.

**Data Provided**:
- 3 years weekly data: sales, marketing spend by channel, external factors
- Channels: TV, Digital, Print, Radio, Promotions
- External factors: seasonality, competitor actions, economic indicators

**Tasks**:
1. **Statistical Modeling** (45 minutes)
   - Build base vs. incremental sales decomposition
   - Implement adstock and saturation curves
   - Validate model performance and assumptions

2. **Scenario Planning** (30 minutes)
   - Create budget optimization recommendations
   - Model different economic scenarios
   - Calculate marginal ROI by channel

3. **Executive Presentation** (15 minutes)
   - Create 3-slide summary for CMO
   - Include actionable recommendations
   - Present uncertainty and confidence intervals

**Success Metrics**:
- Model accuracy (R² > 0.85)
- Business relevance of recommendations
- Statistical validity of approach
- Quality of executive communication

### Week 4-5: Customer Analytics & Segmentation
**Time Allocation**: 2.5 hours
**Format**: Machine learning implementation with strategic application

#### Exercise 3.1: Advanced Customer Segmentation (75 minutes)
**Dataset**: E-commerce customer data (100,000 customers, 2 years history)
- Transaction history, behavioral data, demographic information
- Product categories, seasonal patterns, channel preferences

**Technical Challenge**:
1. **Unsupervised Learning** (35 minutes)
   - Implement K-means and hierarchical clustering
   - Use dimensionality reduction (PCA/t-SNE)
   - Determine optimal number of segments

2. **Segment Characterization** (25 minutes)
   - Profile each segment with key characteristics
   - Calculate segment value and growth potential
   - Identify actionable differences between segments

3. **Marketing Strategy Development** (15 minutes)
   - Create segment-specific value propositions
   - Recommend channel and message strategies
   - Estimate impact on key business metrics

**Deliverables**:
- Technical implementation in Python/R
- Segment profile summaries
- Marketing strategy recommendations

#### Exercise 3.2: Customer Lifetime Value Modeling (75 minutes)
**Objective**: Build predictive CLV model for subscription business.

**Data Provided**:
- Customer acquisition data (demographics, acquisition channel)
- Subscription history (plans, upgrades, downgrades, churn)
- Usage patterns and engagement metrics

**Implementation Steps**:
1. **Model Development** (45 minutes)
   - Implement Buy-Till-You-Die model
   - Build machine learning alternative (Random Forest/XGBoost)
   - Compare model performance and interpretability

2. **Business Application** (20 minutes)
   - Calculate customer acquisition cost thresholds
   - Identify high-value customer characteristics
   - Create retention intervention recommendations

3. **Validation & Monitoring** (10 minutes)
   - Design model validation framework
   - Create monitoring dashboard concepts
   - Plan for model updates and maintenance

**Assessment Focus**:
- Model accuracy and validation approach
- Business insight generation
- Implementation feasibility
- Monitoring and governance considerations

### Week 6-7: Marketing Performance Optimization
**Time Allocation**: 3 hours
**Format**: Optimization modeling with executive dashboard creation

#### Exercise 4.1: Budget Allocation Optimization (105 minutes)
**Scenario**: Optimize $100M marketing budget across 12 channels for Fortune 500 retailer.

**Constraints Provided**:
- Channel-specific budget minimums and maximums
- Seasonal spending requirements
- Brand vs. performance marketing allocation rules
- Competitive response considerations

**Technical Implementation**:
1. **Mathematical Optimization** (50 minutes)

   - Implement diminishing returns curves
   - Include cross-channel interaction effects
   - Solve optimization problem using Python/R

2. **Scenario Analysis** (35 minutes)
   - Model different budget scenarios (+/-20%)
   - Analyze sensitivity to key assumptions
   - Create contingency plans for market changes

3. **Executive Dashboard** (20 minutes)
   - Design interactive dashboard mockup
   - Include key metrics and drill-down capabilities
   - Create alert system for performance deviations

**Deliverables**:
- Optimization model with full documentation
- Budget allocation recommendations
- Executive dashboard design
- Scenario analysis results

**Success Criteria**:
- Mathematical rigor of optimization approach
- Practical feasibility of recommendations
- Quality of scenario planning
- Executive communication effectiveness

#### Exercise 4.2: Real-time Performance Monitoring (75 minutes)
**Challenge**: Design automated monitoring system for marketing performance.

**Requirements**:
- Monitor 50+ KPIs across 15 marketing channels
- Automated anomaly detection and alerting
- Predictive early warning indicators
- Executive reporting automation

**Tasks**:
1. **Monitoring Framework Design** (30 minutes)
   - Define key metrics hierarchy
   - Create alerting rules and thresholds
   - Design escalation procedures

2. **Anomaly Detection Implementation** (30 minutes)
   - Build statistical process control charts
   - Implement machine learning anomaly detection
   - Create false positive reduction mechanisms

3. **Predictive Analytics** (15 minutes)
   - Develop leading indicators
   - Build performance forecasting models
   - Create early warning systems

**Evaluation Focus**:
- Comprehensiveness of monitoring approach
- Technical feasibility of implementation
- Balance between sensitivity and false positives
- Integration with existing systems

## Mid-Term Project: Attribution Model Redesign
**Duration**: 2 weeks (Weeks 6-7)
**Time Investment**: 8-10 hours
**Format**: Individual project with peer review

### Project Overview
Redesign the attribution strategy for a Fortune 500 company transitioning from third-party cookies to privacy-first measurement.

### Company Scenario: MegaRetail Corp
- **Industry**: Omnichannel Retail
- **Revenue**: $25 billion annually
- **Marketing Budget**: $800 million
- **Channels**: 20+ digital and offline channels
- **Challenge**: 40% reduction in trackable conversions due to privacy changes

### Project Requirements

#### Phase 1: Current State Assessment (2-3 hours)
1. **Attribution Audit**
   - Analyze existing attribution methodology
   - Identify gaps and limitations
   - Quantify impact of privacy changes

2. **Data Inventory**
   - Catalog available first-party data sources
   - Assess data quality and coverage
   - Identify integration challenges

3. **Stakeholder Impact Analysis**
   - Map affected business processes
   - Identify change management requirements
   - Assess organizational readiness

#### Phase 2: Future State Design (4-5 hours)
1. **Technical Architecture**
   - Design privacy-first attribution framework
   - Integrate multiple measurement methodologies
   - Plan for real-time and batch processing

2. **Measurement Strategy**
   - Combine attribution, MMM, and incrementality testing
   - Design unified reporting framework
   - Create confidence intervals and uncertainty quantification

3. **Implementation Roadmap**
   - 18-month phased implementation plan
   - Resource requirements and budget estimates
   - Risk mitigation strategies

#### Phase 3: Business Case Development (2 hours)
1. **ROI Analysis**
   - Cost-benefit analysis of new attribution system
   - Revenue impact projections
   - Efficiency gain estimates

2. **Executive Presentation**
   - 15-minute presentation for C-suite
   - Clear recommendations and rationale
   - Implementation timeline and milestones

### Deliverables
1. **Technical Design Document** (15-20 pages)
   - Detailed architecture and methodology
   - Data flow diagrams and technical specifications
   - Integration requirements and dependencies

2. **Business Case Presentation** (10 slides)
   - Executive summary and recommendations
   - ROI analysis and success metrics
   - Implementation plan and resource needs

3. **Implementation Playbook** (5-10 pages)
   - Step-by-step implementation guide
   - Change management recommendations
   - Success metrics and monitoring plan

### Evaluation Rubric
- **Technical Excellence** (35%): Soundness of attribution methodology and architecture
- **Business Impact** (25%): Relevance and value of recommendations
- **Implementation Feasibility** (20%): Realistic timeline and resource planning
- **Executive Communication** (20%): Clarity and persuasiveness of presentation

### Peer Review Process
- Each participant reviews 2 peer submissions
- Structured feedback using provided rubric
- 30-minute virtual discussion sessions
- Incorporation of peer feedback into final submission

## Capstone Project: 3-Year Marketing Analytics Strategy
**Duration**: 3 weeks (Weeks 10-12)
**Time Investment**: 12-15 hours
**Format**: Individual project with executive panel presentation

### Project Objective
Develop a comprehensive 3-year marketing analytics transformation strategy for a Fortune 500 company in your industry (or assigned industry).

### Project Scope
Create a complete strategic plan that addresses:
- Current state assessment and gap analysis
- Future state vision and capabilities roadmap
- Technology transformation strategy
- Organizational change management
- Financial investment and ROI projections
- Implementation timeline and risk management

### Detailed Requirements

#### Section 1: Strategic Assessment (3-4 hours)
1. **Industry & Competitive Analysis**
   - Marketing analytics maturity in your industry
   - Competitive benchmark analysis
   - Emerging trends and disruptions
   - Regulatory and privacy considerations

2. **Current State Analysis**
   - Analytics capabilities maturity assessment
   - Technology stack evaluation
   - Team skills and organizational readiness
   - Data infrastructure and governance

3. **Gap Analysis & Prioritization**
   - Identify critical capability gaps
   - Prioritize improvements by business impact
   - Assess quick wins vs. long-term investments
   - Define success metrics and KPIs

#### Section 2: Future State Vision (3-4 hours)
1. **3-Year Vision Statement**
   - Clear articulation of desired future state
   - Alignment with business strategy and goals
   - Competitive differentiation through analytics
   - Cultural transformation objectives

2. **Capability Roadmap**
   - Detailed capabilities by year
   - Technology platform evolution
   - Advanced analytics and AI integration
   - Real-time decision making capabilities

3. **Operating Model Design**
   - Team structure and roles evolution
   - Process redesign and automation
   - Governance and decision-making frameworks
   - Performance measurement systems

#### Section 3: Implementation Strategy (4-5 hours)
1. **Technology Transformation**
   - Platform selection and integration strategy
   - Data architecture and infrastructure
   - Security and compliance requirements
   - Vendor management and partnerships

2. **Organizational Change Management**
   - Change management strategy and tactics

3. **Financial Planning**

#### Section 4: Risk Management & Governance (2 hours)

2. **Governance Framework**

### Final Deliverables

- Vision and strategic objectives

**Situation Analysis** (8-10 pages)

- 3-year vision and capabilities
- Technology architecture
- Operating model design

**Implementation Plan** (6-8 pages)
- Phased implementation approach
- Resource requirements and timeline
- Change management strategy
- Risk mitigation plans

**Financial Analysis** (3-4 pages)
- Investment requirements by year
- ROI analysis and business case
- Budget allocation and funding
- Sensitivity analysis

#### 2. Executive Presentation (20 minutes + 10 minutes Q&A)
**Slide Structure** (12-15 slides):
1. Executive Summary & Vision
2. Strategic Context & Imperatives
3. Current State Assessment
4. Future State Capabilities
5. Technology Transformation

7. Implementation Roadmap

**Presentation Requirements**:
- Executive-level communication style

#### 3. Implementation Playbook (10-15 pages)
- Detailed first-year action plan
- Month-by-month implementation timeline

## Continuous Assessment Components

### Weekly Discussion Forums (5% of grade)
**Format**: Online discussion with peer interaction
**Requirements**: 
- Initial post (200-300 words) by Wednesday
- Respond to 2 peer posts by Friday
- Share real-world examples and insights

**Sample Topics**:
- "Biggest attribution challenges in your industry"
- "Technology vendor experiences and recommendations"
- "Change management strategies that worked (or failed)"
- "Emerging trends in marketing analytics"

### Case Study Analysis (15% of grade)
**Format**: Written analysis of Fortune 500 case studies
**Length**: 1,000-1,500 words per case study
**Frequency**: 3 case studies throughout course

**Analysis Framework**:
1. **Situation Assessment** (25%)
   - Business context and challenges
   - Strategic objectives and constraints
   - Success criteria definition

2. **Approach Evaluation** (35%)
   - Analytics methodology assessment
   - Technology and implementation choices
   - Organizational change management

3. **Results Analysis** (25%)
   - Quantitative impact assessment
   - Qualitative benefits evaluation
   - Lessons learned identification

4. **Application Planning** (15%)
   - Relevance to your organization
   - Adaptation recommendations
   - Implementation considerations

### Peer Review Exercises (10% of grade)
**Process**: Structured peer feedback using rubrics
**Frequency**: 4 peer reviews throughout course
**Requirements**:
- Constructive feedback using course frameworks
- Specific suggestions for improvement
- Recognition of strengths and innovations
- Professional and respectful communication

**Benefits**:
- Exposure to diverse approaches and industries
- Development of evaluation and feedback skills
- Building professional network within cohort
- Learning from peer innovations and mistakes
