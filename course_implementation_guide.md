# Course Implementation Guide
## Advanced Digital Marketing Analytics Course for Fortune 500 Directors

---

## Implementation Overview

### Course Delivery Model
**Hybrid Learning Approach**: Combines self-paced online modules, live virtual workshops, peer collaboration, and hands-on projects to maximize learning effectiveness for senior marketing professionals.

### Target Cohort Size
- **Optimal**: 20-25 participants per cohort
- **Minimum**: 12 participants (ensures viable peer interactions)
- **Maximum**: 30 participants (maintains quality of personalized attention)

### Instructor Requirements
- **Lead Instructor**: PhD in Marketing/Statistics + 10+ years Fortune 500 marketing analytics experience
- **Technical Instructors**: 2-3 specialists with deep expertise in attribution, customer analytics, and optimization
- **Industry Practitioners**: 5-7 guest speakers who are current Fortune 500 marketing analytics directors

---

## Pre-Course Setup (8 weeks before start)

### Participant Selection & Prerequisites

#### Application Process
1. **Application Form** (30 minutes)
   - Current role and responsibilities
   - Years of marketing analytics experience
   - Technical skill self-assessment
   - Learning objectives and career goals
   - Company size and industry

2. **Technical Skills Assessment** (45 minutes)
   - SQL proficiency test (basic to intermediate)
   - Excel/Google Sheets advanced functions
   - Statistical concepts understanding
   - Data visualization capabilities
   - Basic Python or R exposure (preferred, not required)

3. **Leadership Experience Evaluation**
   - Team size and structure
   - Budget responsibility level
   - Cross-functional collaboration experience
   - Executive presentation experience

#### Prerequisite Skills
**Required**:
- 5+ years marketing analytics experience
- Advanced Excel/Google Sheets proficiency
- Basic SQL query writing ability
- Experience with at least one analytics platform (GA, Adobe Analytics, etc.)
- Team leadership experience (3+ people)

**Preferred**:
- Python or R programming exposure
- Statistical analysis experience
- Marketing automation platform experience
- Data visualization tool experience (Tableau, Power BI)
- MBA or quantitative graduate degree

### Technology Platform Setup

#### Learning Management System (LMS)
**Platform**: Canvas Enterprise or similar enterprise LMS
**Features Required**:
- Video content delivery with progress tracking
- Assignment submission and grading
- Discussion forums with threading
- Peer review workflows
- Integration with external tools
- Mobile accessibility
- SCORM compliance for external content

#### Virtual Classroom Platform
**Platform**: Zoom Pro or Microsoft Teams Premium
**Configuration**:
- Breakout room capabilities for 30+ participants
- Recording and transcription features
- Screen sharing and whiteboard tools
- Chat functionality with file sharing
- Integration with LMS for seamless access

#### Collaboration Platform
**Platform**: Microsoft Teams or Slack Enterprise
**Setup**:
- Course-wide channels for announcements and general discussion
- Module-specific channels for focused discussions
- Breakout channels for group projects
- Direct messaging for peer connections
- File sharing and document collaboration

#### Technical Environment Setup
**Cloud Infrastructure**:
- AWS or Google Cloud accounts for all participants
- Pre-configured environments with required software
- Shared datasets in secure cloud storage
- VPN access for enterprise security requirements

**Software Licenses**:
- Tableau Desktop licenses (12-month duration)
- Python/R environments (Anaconda or RStudio Cloud)
- Statistical software access (SPSS, SAS, or Stata)
- Marketing platform sandbox accounts

### Content Preparation

#### Video Content Production
**Total Video Content**: 20+ hours across all modules
**Production Requirements**:
- Professional studio quality (4K resolution minimum)
- Interactive elements (clickable hotspots, quizzes)
- Closed captioning and transcripts
- Multiple format exports (streaming, download, mobile)
- Average video length: 10-15 minutes per segment

#### Dataset Preparation
**Anonymization Process**:
- Partner with 3-5 Fortune 500 companies for real datasets
- Complete data anonymization and GDPR compliance review
- Legal agreements for educational use
- Quality assurance and documentation
- Cloud storage setup with appropriate access controls

#### Assessment Development
**Question Banks**:
- 500+ multiple choice questions across all topics
- 100+ practical scenario questions
- Rubrics for all major projects and presentations
- Peer review templates and guidelines
- Automated grading setup for technical exercises

## Weekly Implementation Rhythm

### Standard Week Structure

#### Monday: Content Release & Goal Setting
**Activities**:
- New module content becomes available
- Weekly goals and milestones communication
- Live Q&A session for previous week's content (30 minutes)
- Technical announcements and updates

#### Tuesday-Wednesday: Self-Paced Learning
**Participant Activities**:
- Video content consumption (1-2 hours)
- Reading assignments and research
- Individual exercises and practice problems
- Discussion forum participation

**Instructor Activities**:
- Forum monitoring and response
- Individual progress tracking
- Content updates based on feedback
- Preparation for live sessions

#### Thursday: Live Workshop Session (90 minutes)
**Format**:
- **Opening** (10 minutes): Week recap and objectives
- **Interactive Lecture** (30 minutes): Deep dive on key concepts
- **Breakout Activities** (35 minutes): Small group problem solving
- **Group Discussion** (10 minutes): Insights sharing and synthesis
- **Closing** (5 minutes): Next steps and assignments

#### Friday: Practical Application & Peer Review
**Activities**:
- Assignment submissions due
- Peer review assignments distributed
- Office hours for assignment support
- Week wrap-up and reflection

#### Weekend: Reflection & Preparation
**Participant Activities**:
- Peer review completion
- Reflection on week's learning
- Preparation for upcoming content
- Optional supplementary reading

### Live Session Management

#### Pre-Session Preparation (24 hours before)
**Instructor Checklist**:
- Review participant questions and discussion topics
- Prepare interactive elements and breakout activities
- Test all technology and backup systems
- Send reminder emails with agenda and materials
- Prepare polls, quizzes, and engagement activities

#### Session Facilitation Best Practices
**Engagement Techniques**:
- Start with industry-relevant warm-up questions
- Use polling and real-time feedback tools
- Incorporate breakout rooms for small group discussions
- Share screen for collaborative problem-solving
- Encourage camera use for better engagement

**Time Management**:
- Strict adherence to scheduled timing
- Built-in breaks for longer sessions
- Buffer time for technical issues
- Clear transitions between activities
- Parking lot for questions that need follow-up

#### Post-Session Activities
**Immediate Follow-up** (within 2 hours):
- Session recording processing and upload
- Chat transcript and resource sharing
- Follow-up email with key takeaways
- Individual feedback collection

**Weekly Analysis** (within 24 hours):
- Attendance and engagement metrics review
- Participant feedback analysis
- Content effectiveness assessment
- Next week's content adjustments

## Assessment Implementation

### Continuous Assessment Management

#### Weekly Exercise Grading
**Process**:
- Automated grading for technical components (50% of exercises)
- Instructor review for strategic and communication components
- Peer review integration for collaborative learning
- Feedback delivery within 48 hours of submission

**Quality Assurance**:
- Standardized rubrics for all assessments
- Multiple instructor review for major assignments
- Calibration sessions for consistent grading
- Student appeals process for grade disputes

#### Peer Review Facilitation
**Assignment Distribution**:
- Automated random assignment of peer reviews
- Diversity considerations (industry, experience level)
- Anonymous review process to encourage honest feedback
- Multiple reviews per submission for reliability

**Quality Control**:
- Instructor review of peer feedback quality
- Training sessions on effective peer review
- Recognition for outstanding peer reviewers
- Intervention for inadequate or inappropriate feedback
